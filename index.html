Legal Awareness: Key Issues

The legal system develops daily. Organizations must address these 4 key issues to avoid damages and protect their brand.

1. Net Neutrality

The concept that ISPs should treat all content equally, without blocking or "fast lanes". In the Philippines, ISPs often violate this by offering "free data" packages (like free Facebook) to influence user behavior.

2. Data Protection

Partners and clients expect their data to be private. Working with personal info requires transparency. Failure to protect data like the Facebook & Cambridge Analytica scandal can lead to legal repercussions.

3. IP Theft

Intellectual Property theft involves stealing ideas, designs, or software. This includes "Patent Trolling," where companies buy patents just to sue others, like the case where Apple had to pay $500 million for FaceTime infringements.

4. Non-Compete Laws

Agreements that prohibit employees from moving to a rival company after resigning. While this protects trade secrets, the tech industry often opposes it because it stifles innovation.

How to Avoid Legal Problems

  • Communication: Document expectations and timeframes clearly. Always keep email confirmations.
  • Pick Your Battles: Own your failures. It is cheaper to apologize than to go to court.
  • Legal Advice: Do not rely on the internet. Get advice from a qualified, experienced lawyer.
  • Follow Through: Perform according to expectations and keep proof of performance (receipts/emails).

The 5 Scopes of Cyber Protection

Cyber protection integrates Cybersecurity with Data Protection to address modern threats.

1. Safety

Ensuring a reliable copy of data is always available. If the original is corrupted, a backup ensures time isn't wasted on recovery.

2. Accessibility

Ensuring data is available from any location at any time. Ideally, copies should be stored on both physical devices and cloud storage.

3. Privacy

Control over who can view data. You must limit access to trusted people to prevent "insider attacks".

4. Authenticity

Ensuring the backup is an exact, unmodified copy of the original. This prevents the spread of wrong or tampered information.

5. Security

Protecting systems against malicious threats. This involves monitoring data flow and installing antivirus software to stop malware.
